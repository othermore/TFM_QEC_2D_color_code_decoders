    \caption{Topological Color Code representations (Distance 7, 4.8.8 lattice).
    In the Primal Graph (b), physical qubits are nodes and detectors are faces (octagons/squares).
    In the Dual Graph (c), the geometry is inverted: detectors become nodes and physical qubits are represented as the hyperedges connecting them. Bulk qubits connecting 3 detectors are shown as triangles. Boundary qubits connecting 2 detectors are depicted as lens-shaped digons, and corner qubits connecting to a single detector are shown as petal-shaped loops attached to their respective node.
    This figure is a simplified illustration used to explain the Projection and Restriction decoders; it does not depict a full color‑code lattice. The Möbius decoder gains its advantage by handling these dangling boundary qubits and connecting them to additional detectors across the lattice.}
